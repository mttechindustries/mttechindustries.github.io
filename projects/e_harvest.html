E_Harvest

Multi-Modal Ambient Energy Harvesting

A breakthrough energy harvesting architecture using enhanced Galfenol (Fe-Ga) magnetostrictive materials to simultaneously capture energy from orthogonal ambient sources — vibration, atmospheric pressure gradients, thermal differentials, and solar — without source competition.

Core Innovation

Enhanced Galfenol Magnetostrictive Architecture

E_Harvest centers on a proprietary processing breakthrough for Galfenol (Fe₀.₈₂Ga₀.₁₈) — achieving 4,500 ppm strain, 3× the performance of conventional Terfenol-D (1,500 ppm baseline). The enhanced material operates across a dramatically wider temperature range and introduces a ductility transformation that makes large-scale deployment practical for the first time.

Orthogonal Energy Source Architecture

Simultaneous Non-Competing Capture

The key insight of E_Harvest is that ambient energy sources — vibration, pressure gradient, thermal, and solar — operate at different frequencies and physical mechanisms. They are orthogonal: capturing one does not diminish the others. E_Harvest's architecture harvests all four simultaneously from a single integrated system.

Vibration Harvesting (1–2,000 Hz)

Power density: 1.2 W/cm³ at 75% efficiency. Magnetostrictive conversion from structural vibration, machinery, traffic, and acoustic sources.

Pressure Gradient (100–10,000 Pa)

Exploits atmospheric pressure differentials across altitude changes (~12 kPa per 1,000m). Passive capture from building ventilation, wind, and barometric fluctuation.

Thermal Differential (5–50°C ΔT)

8% thermoelectric baseline efficiency, enhanced by Galfenol's expanded temperature operating range. Captures waste heat from machinery, sunlight, and environmental gradients.

Solar (1,000 W/m² irradiance)

35% efficiency perovskite cell integration. Complementary to other sources — operates when vibration and pressure sources are quiescent.

Technical Architecture

Magnetostrictive Energy Conversion Model

The core power equation driving E_Harvest's vibration harvesting: P = 0.5 × E × ε² × f where E is elastic modulus, ε is magnetostrictive strain, and f is frequency. Enhanced Galfenol's 3× strain improvement directly translates to a 9× power density advantage over Terfenol-D-based systems at equivalent frequency.
